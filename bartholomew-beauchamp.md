# Bartholomew Byron Beauchamp

**B.B. Beauchamp** - *Adventures Most Perilous*

## Role

An unreliable narrator used to establish the campaign world through vignettes. Both players and in-world characters encounter the setting through his tales. He serves as a framing device, world-revealer, and tone-setter.

## Character

- **Profession:** Writer of serialized tales published in city broadsheets
- **Reputation:** Dismissed as drivel by the respectable, secretly devoured by many. Has a growing following among both the downtrodden and the affluent. His fame is real but sparse - most people he meets while traveling have never heard of him.
- **Motivation:** Genuine belief that magnificent tales happen and deserve to be shared. He is earnest, not cynical.
- **Background:** Deliberately obscure. Independently wealthy enough to travel freely, but his origins and station remain unrevealed.

## Method

Travels to remote regions and tiny settlements in the hinterlands to interview people and collect stories. Periodically returns to the larger cities where the printing presses operate to deliver his latest batch of tales.

He embellishes. He distorts toward grandeur, making things larger than they were. He's not lying - he's revealing the *spirit* of the thing. His versions of events often replace what people half-remember, becoming the accepted truth.

## Medium

Publishes vignettes in broadsheets - large single-page affairs printed in the larger cities, some daily. His tales sit alongside news, proclamations, trade prices, and notices. Fiction and fact share the same page.

The work is ephemeral and disposable. Broadsheets are read, passed around, used to wrap goods, burned for kindling. But somehow the tales persist - someone always saves a copy, tavern walls get papered with them, stories that captured something true get remembered.

## World Implications

- Printing presses exist but are rare, concentrated in larger cities with jealous guilds
- Urban centers are bustling commercial places (late 14th century feel) with literate merchants, clerks, and officials
- Remote regions remain sparsely populated with huge open unpopulated lands between settlements
- The tales spread outward through merchants and peddlers, moving slower than rumor but faster than history

## Inspiration

W.W. Beauchamp from *Unforgiven* (the name is an easter egg for players to discover). A tale-collector rather than a scholarly chronicler like Volothamp Geddarm - he tells tales rather than recording ecology and history.
